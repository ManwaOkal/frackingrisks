Fracking Near Home

What New Homeowners Need to Know

1Know What You're Moving Into

Hydraulic fracturing (fracking) uses high-pressure fluid to break apart deep rock layers and release natural gas or oil. Wells can be drilled within a mile of residential areas.

What actually happens during fracking:

  • Millions of gallons of water mixed with chemicals are injected underground
  • Rock fractures release trapped gas or oil
  • Wastewater returns to the surface and requires disposal
  • Operations can continue for years at a single site

2Energy Benefits Come with Tradeoffs

Fracking brings real economic advantages to local communities: job creation, increased tax revenue, and lower energy costs. Many of your neighbors may work in the industry or benefit from it financially.

These benefits exist alongside environmental considerations. Industrial activity, truck traffic, and potential contamination risks can affect property values and quality of life in residential areas.

Understanding both aspects helps you make decisions that align with your priorities as a homeowner.

Potential Impacts on Daily Life

Water Quality

What could happen

Chemical additives and wastewater can contaminate groundwater through spills or well-casing failures.

Who is more vulnerable

Private wells face higher risk than municipal water systems with treatment facilities.

What makes detection difficult

Contamination may not be immediately visible or detectable without testing.

Air Quality

What could happen

Diesel emissions from trucks and drilling equipment, plus methane and volatile organic compounds released during operations.

Who is more vulnerable

Children and those with asthma face higher risk of respiratory effects.

What makes detection difficult

Air quality changes may be gradual and require monitoring equipment to detect.

Property

What could happen

Proximity to active wells may affect home resale value. Noise and light pollution from 24-hour operations, plus road damage from heavy truck traffic.

What makes detection difficult

Property value impacts may not be apparent until you try to sell, and effects vary significantly by location and distance from operations.

3Protect Your Family's Health

Health risks linked to fracking operations include:

  • Respiratory issues from air pollutants
  • Skin and eye irritation from chemical exposure
  • Potential impacts on pregnancy and child development
  • Mental health effects from noise and community stress

If you're raising children or planning to start a family, these risks deserve your attention. Young children and pregnant individuals face higher vulnerability to environmental exposures.

Research on health impacts continues to evolve, and individual outcomes vary based on many factors including proximity, duration of exposure, and personal health status.

4Take Action: What You Can Do

A. Immediate Personal Actions

  • Get a baseline water quality test before any nearby drilling begins
  • Test again if operations start near your home
  • Keep records of all test results

B. Staying Informed

  • Attend local town hall meetings about drilling permits
  • Join neighborhood groups focused on environmental monitoring
  • Follow state environmental agency updates for your county

C. Civic & Legal Options

  • Understand property rights and mineral rights in your area
  • Learn about disclosure requirements for nearby drilling
  • Research your state's regulations on setback distances
  • Contact local representatives about environmental concerns
  • Support comprehensive pre-drilling water testing requirements
  • Push for transparent chemical disclosure policies

5Common Questions

Can fracking contaminate my drinking water?

+

Yes, contamination can occur through surface spills, wastewater disposal, or compromised well casings. Private wells face higher risk than municipal systems with treatment facilities.

How do I find out about drilling near my home?

+

Check your state's oil and gas commission website for active permits and well locations. Most states maintain public databases of drilling activity.

Will fracking affect my property value?

+

Studies show mixed results. Proximity to active wells can decrease property values, while economic benefits in the broader region may increase them. Effects vary by location and distance.

What chemicals are used in fracking?

+

Hundreds of chemicals may be used, including acids, biocides, and friction reducers. Some states require disclosure, others do not. Specific mixtures are often considered trade secrets.

Is there fracking near me?

+

Use your state's online mapping tools to locate active and permitted wells. Enter your address to see operations within a specific radius of your home.
